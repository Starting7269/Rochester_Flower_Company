About Rochester Flower Company

This isn’t a brand or a business. It’s just one grower in Rochester, NY doing small-batch craft cannabis with intention, patience, and care — and quietly sharing it with adults who genuinely benefit from it.

Hi, I’m the grower

Rochester Flower Company is the name I slapped onto my overbuilt home grow — a couple of tents, too many spreadsheets, and a slightly obsessive attention to detail. I’m not a team, not a brand, not a company. Just a person who grows small batches of clean, reliable flower.

I started RFC because I kept meeting people who used cannabis the same way others use an inhaler, a sleep aid, or a heating pad — as real medicine — yet were constantly priced out or forced to settle for questionable quality.

And honestly? A lot of commercial flower didn’t feel great. Heavy salts, questionable pesticides, whatever happened in transit… once you start paying attention to how it’s grown, it’s hard to unsee.

What I actually do

A few times a year, I run a carefully dialed-in indoor grow. When harvest comes, the flower is slow dried, properly cured, and quietly gifted to adults who truly use cannabis for relief — no storefront, no drops, no hype.

  • No online shop.
  • No pricing.
  • No “product launches.”

Just plants, people, and a small bit of mutual aid.

What matters to me

  • People first — This project exists to help someone sleep, relax, ease pain, or get through a hard day.
  • Craft over hype — Clean inputs, consistency, and transparency matter more than trends.
  • Honesty — I’ll always be clear about what this is and what it isn’t. I’m not pretending to be a dispensary.
  • Respect — For the plant, for the law, and for the people who trust me with their medicine.

What this actually costs

Running a proper indoor grow isn’t cheap. Between seeds, nutrients, supplements, electricity, coco/perlite, water treatment, and consumables, each indoor plant costs roughly $150–$200 to grow — not counting the original ~$2,000 equipment investment.

I typically run 12–16 indoor plants a year, putting annual indoor costs somewhere between $1,800 and $3,200.

Outdoor plants are cheaper but far riskier. Weather, pests, mold, bad luck — I’ve lost whole plants and entire seasons. Outdoor runs average $50–$75 per plant, or about $300–$450 annually.

Is this legal?

Yes — entirely. New York law allows adults 21+ to gift up to 3 ounces of cannabis flower or 1 ounce of concentrate to another adult 21+. There can be no payment, trades, exchanges of value, or reciprocal favors involved. Gifting must occur only in places where possession is legal.

This is all established under the MRTA and codified in NY Penal Law § 222.05 .

What this project is (and isn’t)

Rochester Flower Company is a personal, small-scale home grow. Cannabis is not sold here. If cannabis is ever gifted, it is only:

  • To adults 21+ under New York’s personal cultivation and gifting laws
  • Without any payment, trade, service, or exchange of value
  • Only in places where cannabis possession is legal

Any support provided through Buy Me a Coffee is a voluntary donation to support the garden and content, not payment for cannabis, products, or services. No medical advice is offered, and nothing here is intended to diagnose, treat, or cure any condition.

Nothing on this page is legal or financial advice — it’s simply transparency about what this project is and isn’t.
