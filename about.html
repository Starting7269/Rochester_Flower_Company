About Rochester Flower Company

This isn’t a brand launch. It’s just my home grow, a lot of love, and some flower that keeps landing in the hands of people who need it.

Hi, I’m the grower

Rochester Flower Company is the fancy name I gave to my overbuilt home grow in Rochester, NY. It’s small, personal, and run with a mix of spreadsheets, timers, and a slightly ridiculous amount of care.

I like to say: my shadow touches more than my hand. I notice everything; leaf curl, air flow, plant posture, and only intervene when the plants actually need something.

I started RFC because I kept running into people using cannabis as medicine, for sleep, pain, anxiety, appetite, but often having to choose between bills or buds. That didn’t feel right.

On top of cost, lots of commercial grows use inputs that felt… grim. Chemicals, questionable pesticides, possible heavy-metal residue. Once you start paying attention, it gets harder to feel good about that ending up in someone’s jar.

What I actually do

A few times a year I run a small, carefully dialed-in grow. When it's harvest time, the flower gets trimmed, cured, and quietly gifted to adults who genuinely need medicine — no fanfare, no menu, no markup.

  • No online store.
  • No price list.
  • No “drops.”

Just plants, people, and a little bit of mutual aid.

What matters to me

  • People first — The point is to help someone get through a rough night or a rough day.
  • Craft over hype — Clean inputs, consistent process, and trust over trends.
  • Honesty — Clear about what this is and what it isn’t. No pretending to be a dispensary.
  • Respect — For the plant, for the law, and for the folks who trust me with their medicine.

How much does this cost?

Quite a bit. This is the less glamorous side of the project. Each indoor plant costs approximately $150–$200 when you factor in seeds, nutrients, supplements, electricity, grow medium, pH adjustments, and other consumables. This figure excludes my initial equipment investment of about $2,000 and any labor. I typically grow around 12-16 indoor plants per year, so assuming no equipment failures, my indoor grow costs fall between $1,800 and $3,200 annually. More likely closer to the $3k.

Outdoor grows are cheaper but much riskier. I’ve lost full plants and entire crops to things that never happen indoors. Each outdoor plant costs about $50–$75, so with six plants a year, I spend roughly $300–$450.

Is this legal?

Doh! I knew I was forgetting something—just kidding. Yes, this is completely legal. Adults 21 and over may gift another adult 21 or older up to 3 ounces (84 grams) of cannabis flower or 1 ounce (24 grams) of concentrate. There can be no payment, trade, exchange of value, or reciprocal favors involved. The transfer must occur in a place where cannabis possession is legal (so not in schools or similar restricted locations). All of this is established by the MRTA and codified in NY Penal Law § 222.05
