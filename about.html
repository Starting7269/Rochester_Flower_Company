Craft cannabis for real people

Rochester Flower Company isn't a brand or a business. It's just one grower in Rochester, NY working in small batches to make good flower accessible to people who genuinely rely on it.

No storefront, no hype. Just plants, craft, and community.

Why this exists

This started because I saw people in my life struggling to afford the thing that helped them function, sleep, heal, or feel like themselves. Meanwhile, the legal market flooded with corporate branding, high prices, and flower that didn't feel like medicine.

Some commercial growers don't dry, cure, or store properly. They let the terpenes off-gas, then spray synthetic terpenes back on to fake quality. That's not medicine. That's not cool.

So I grow the way I wish the world grew: small batches, clean inputs, honest curing, no shortcuts, and no sales. Just trustworthy flower, shared responsibly with adults who need it.

Who this is for (and who it isn't)

This is for you if:

  • You use cannabis as real medicine
  • You appreciate well-grown flower
  • You value honesty and transparency
  • You can't always afford dispensary prices
  • You believe craft matters

This isn't for you if:

  • You're under 21 (strictly enforced)
  • You're looking for commercial transactions
  • You're seeking medical advice or diagnosis
  • You're uncomfortable with the personal nature of gifting
  • You're building a stash rather than using for genuine need

Where I operate

I operate exclusively in Rochester, NY and surrounding areas where I can personally verify compliance and build genuine relationships. This isn't scalable, and that's intentional. This is a garden, not a distribution network.

The human behind the plants

It's just me: a husband, a pet dad, a spreadsheet addict, and a slightly obsessive plant nerd. What started as a hobby became a personal mission. Grow the best flower I can, share it responsibly, and make the whole thing feel human again.

Everything you see comes from one pair of hands, in one home, in one city.

What's next

I don't have plans to scale or turn this into something commercial. The goal is simple: keep the garden healthy, keep learning, keep helping, and keep sharing.

If I could dream for a moment, I'd love to see my passion for helping animals and providing medicinal plants collide. Imagine craft flower grown using composted output from rescued animals, with proceeds feeding and caring for those animals. That's a world I want to live in.

Thanks for being here. Truly.
