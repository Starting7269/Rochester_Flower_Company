How I Grow

Short version: way too much effort for the tiny amount of flower that leaves this room. Long version: keep scrolling.

Environment & medium

  • Indoor tents with LED lighting tuned for cannabis.
  • Coco coir + perlite for a fast, oxygen-rich root zone.
  • Autopots and air domes so the roots never have to guess about water or air.

The idea is simple: give the plant a stable, predictable world so it can focus on being its best self instead of surviving my mistakes.

Feeding & general plant babysitting

  • Balanced, cannabis-focused nutrients with a “less but consistent” mindset.
  • Monitor pH, EC, and VPD like a complete nerd.
  • Gentle training—no plant torture, just shaping for light and airflow.

I’m always trying to hit that sweet spot where the plants look happy, not stressed, and the leaves are praying but not screaming.

Drying

When it’s chop time, plants get hung whole in a separate, controlled space. I aim for around 60°F and 60% relative humidity as often as real life allows.

Fast, hot dries are harsh and sad. Slow, cool dries keep terpenes and make the smoke something your lungs don’t resent you for.

Curing

Once the outsides feel dry and the stems give that “almost snap,” I buck the flower down and move it into curing containers in a stable, cool environment.

This is where everything smooths out and the flower becomes something you actually want in a jar, not just “technically dried plant matter.”

Why bother with all this?

Because the people who end up with this flower are using it for real things: sleep, pain, anxiety, appetite, not just “a fun Saturday.” They deserve something grown with intention, not whatever was left over on a bulk pallet.

Craft doesn’t mean perfect. It just means I’m trying as hard as I can to make every run better than the last.
