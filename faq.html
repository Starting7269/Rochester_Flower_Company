Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about how Rochester Flower Company operates, the gifting process, quality standards, and the legal framework in New York.

The Basics

Rochester Flower Company operates under New York's personal cultivation laws. I grow cannabis for personal use and gift small amounts to adults 21+ who would benefit from it. There's no transaction, no sale, no exchange of money.

If you're interested in receiving, you can fill out the contact form. However, I'm currently at capacity and not accepting new recipients. When space opens up, I'll reach out to people who've expressed interest.

I rotate strains based on what reputable breeders are offering and what I think will be interesting to grow. Recent grows have included Chocolate Thai, Slamberry, and Papaya Bomb crosses.

I prioritize genetics from well-known, trusted breeders who lab test their seeds. You can follow current grows on the Plants blog to see what's in the tents.

Quality starts with genetics—I only use reputable breeders. From there, it's about environment: controlled temperature, humidity, proper lighting, clean growing medium, and consistent feeding.

I dry slowly at 60°F and 60% relative humidity, cure properly in Grove Bags, and track everything. No pesticides, no cutting corners. Check the Craft Process page for details on how I grow.

Growing & Process

Scale: I grow 6-12 plants at a time. Dispensaries grow hundreds or thousands. Small batches mean more attention per plant.

Priorities: Dispensaries optimize for yield and shelf life. I optimize for terpene preservation and proper cure. I'm not trying to maximize profit—I'm trying to grow something I'd actually want to smoke.

Transparency: You can literally read my grow journals and see exactly how your flower was grown. Try getting that from a commercial operation.

It depends on the strain and whether it's photoperiod or autoflower, but generally:

Photoperiod strains: 3-5 months total (3-6 weeks veg, 8-10 weeks flower, 2-3 weeks dry, 2-4 weeks cure minimum)

Autoflowers: 2-3 months total (no veg control—they flower automatically based on age)

Then there's the cure. Technically smokable after dry, but good after 2-4 weeks in the jar, and great after 2-3 months.

Legal & Compliance

Yes. New York's Marijuana Regulation and Taxation Act (MRTA) allows adults 21+ to cultivate up to 6 plants (3 mature, 3 immature) for personal use, or 12 plants per household if multiple adults live there.

Personal cultivation for gifting (not selling) to other adults 21+ is permitted under NY Penal Law § 222.05. I don't sell anything—everything is gifted, and I stay well within legal plant counts.

That said, I'm not a lawyer and this isn't legal advice. Laws vary by jurisdiction. Always verify your local laws before cultivating.
