Privacy Policy

Last updated: December 27, 2025

This isn't a data harvesting operation. I'm just one person with some tents and plants. Here's what happens to your information when you contact me.

What Information I Collect

If you use the contact form, I collect what you give me:

  • Your name
  • Your email address
  • Whatever you write in the message field
  • Technical information: Your IP address and browser user agent (collected automatically for security and record-keeping)

I also use sessionStorage (browser-based, temporary) to remember that you've verified your age. This clears automatically when you close your browser.

That's it. I don't use cookies, tracking pixels, or analytics scripts beyond basic Cloudflare security features.

How I Use Your Information

Your contact information is used for:

  • Responding to your message - the primary purpose
  • Legal compliance and record-keeping - contact form submissions are stored for business record-keeping purposes and potential legal compliance in the cannabis industry
  • Security - IP addresses help identify spam and abuse

I don't:

  • Sell your information
  • Share it with third parties (except the services mentioned below that process the form)
  • Use it for marketing
  • Add you to email lists without permission

How Your Data is Stored

When you submit the contact form, here's what happens:

  1. Submission processing: Your form data is processed by a Cloudflare Worker (a secure serverless function running on Cloudflare's network)
  2. Email notification: I receive an email notification via Resend (email delivery service)
  3. Permanent storage: Your submission is stored in a private GitHub repository as a CSV record for business and legal record-keeping purposes

Important: Unlike typical contact forms that delete data after sending an email, your submission is permanently stored in a private repository. This is for legal protection and business record-keeping in the cannabis industry, where documentation of communications can be important.

Third-Party Services

I use these services to make the contact form work:

Cloudflare Workers (Form Processing)

Contact form submissions are processed through Cloudflare Workers, a serverless computing platform. The Worker runs on Cloudflare's secure network and handles form validation, email sending, and data storage.

Cloudflare Privacy Policy

Resend (Email Delivery)

Email notifications are sent using Resend, a transactional email service. Resend processes your name, email, and message to deliver the notification to me, but does not permanently store your data.

Resend Privacy Policy

GitHub (Data Storage)

Contact form submissions are stored in a private GitHub repository for record-keeping purposes. GitHub provides the storage infrastructure, but only I have access to this private repository. Your data is stored as structured CSV records.

GitHub Privacy Statement

Cloudflare (Security & DNS)

The entire site uses Cloudflare for security, DDoS protection, and DNS management. They may collect technical information like IP addresses for security purposes.

Cloudflare Privacy Policy

GitHub Pages (Hosting)

The website is hosted on GitHub Pages. They may collect technical information for service operation.

GitHub Privacy Statement

Data Retention

Contact form submissions: Stored indefinitely in a private GitHub repository for business record-keeping and legal compliance purposes. This is intentional and necessary for operating in the cannabis industry where documentation of communications may be legally relevant.

Age verification: Stored in your browser's sessionStorage only. Automatically deleted when you close your browser. Not transmitted or stored on any server.

Email notifications: Retained in my email inbox according to my email retention practices. Typically deleted or archived after the inquiry is resolved.

If you want your contact form submission deleted from the GitHub repository, see "Your Rights" below.

Your Rights

You can request that I delete your contact form submission from my records by contacting me. Note that I may need to retain certain records for legal compliance.

Security

I take reasonable precautions to protect your information:

  • HTTPS encryption: Your connection to the site is secure
  • Cloudflare security: DDoS protection, Web Application Firewall, and security headers
  • Private repository: Contact form data is stored in a private GitHub repository with access restricted to me only
  • API key authentication: All connections between services use secure API keys
  • Spam protection: Honeypot fields and rate limiting to prevent abuse
  • Minimal data collection: I only collect what's necessary

That said, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. Don't send me your Social Security number, credit card info, passwords, or other sensitive personal data. The contact form is for basic business inquiries, not sensitive information.

Children's Privacy

This site is for adults 21 and older only. I don't knowingly collect information from anyone under 21.

If you're under 21, you shouldn't be here, and you definitely shouldn't be filling out the contact form.

If I become aware that someone under 21 has submitted the contact form, I will delete their information immediately.

Why I Store Contact Form Data

You might wonder why I permanently store contact form submissions when many websites don't. Here's why:

  • Legal protection: In the cannabis industry, having records of all communications can be important for demonstrating compliance with gifting laws and responding to any legal inquiries
  • Business records: Documentation of inquiries, partnerships, and business communications
  • Transparency: I'd rather be upfront about keeping records than pretend I delete everything
  • Spam tracking: Identifying patterns of abuse or spam attempts

This is a small personal operation, not a corporation. The data is stored in a private repository that only I can access. I'm not analyzing it, selling it, or doing anything with it beyond what's described here.

Changes to This Policy

If I make changes to this privacy policy, I'll update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page.

Significant changes (like adding new third-party services or changing data retention practices) will be noted with a brief summary of what changed.

Contact About Privacy

If you have questions about this privacy policy or how I handle your information, you can reach me through the contact form (yes, the irony is noted) or find another way to reach me if you prefer not to use the form.

I'm just one person running a home grow, not a legal department, but I'll do my best to answer your questions and handle your data responsibly.
